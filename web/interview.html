Would you also like to participate in an optional follow-up interview (via chat or telephone)?

You can give us your name, telephone number or email, for a follow-up telephone interview after you complete today's search. This optional follow-up interview is confidential, not anonymous. The telephone interview might contain questions that you may find sensitive or of a personal nature about the search or searches you have just completed.

One potential risk of participating in the follow-up interview is that confidential information about you may be accidentally disclosed outside of the research team. We will use our best efforts to keep the information you provide secure, and we think the risk of accidental disclosure is very small. A link between your personal information, such as name, phone number and the country from which you are accessing the arXiv, and the data we collect will be kept to allow for follow-up to complete the interview and to mail the payment for completing the interview.

However, after we have processed your telephone/chat interview we will sever the link between your personal information and the data you gave us. We would like to record the audio of the telephone interview in order to be able to review it and make notes. As soon as we process your telephone interview, we will destroy the audio recording, keeping only the anonymous notes. Audio recording your interview renders the data collection confidential, not anonymous.

We will pay you $50, in the form of a gift card, for a completed interview (or an equivalent amount in a foreign currency if you do not reside in the U.S.). If you decide to stop partway through an interview you will receive a gift card for the fraction of the interview that has been completed.

We hope you can give us about 20 minutes of your time to help our research. We may do the telephone interview in one longer call, or in two shorter ones in the same week, if you prefer.

There are no foreseeable risks to participating in this research. Your participation in our study is completely voluntary, and refusing to participate will involve no penalty. You may stop the interview at any time without penalty.
